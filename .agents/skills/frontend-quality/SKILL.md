name: frontend-quality
description: Design and review CivicLens responsive UI for a polished hackathon demo without generic AI-dashboard patterns. Use for React components, layouts, copy, accessibility, states, and browser verification.
---

Preserve the ink/paper/marigold/teal identity, but remove fake device chrome and cramped mobile-only desktop layouts.

Before declaring a screen complete:
- Verify 360px mobile, tablet, and desktop layouts.
- Use a clear hierarchy and one dominant action.
- Body text is readable; touch targets are at least 44x44.
- Keyboard, focus, labels, dialogs, and status announcements work.
- Loading, empty, error, permission, low-confidence, and retry states exist.
- Copy is plain, truthful, and does not imply government submission.
- Agent evidence and human approval boundaries are understandable.
- Avoid excessive pills, tiny uppercase text, decorative fake telemetry, and gratuitous animation.
